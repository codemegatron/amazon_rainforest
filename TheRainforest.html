The Amazon Rainforest

Some Elementary Facts

The Amazon rainforest is 2.1 million square miles of land. Brazil has 60% of it, Columbia has 10% and Peru has 13%. Some other countries have small parts within, but at maximum 5%.Altogether, nine nations have part of the rainforest in them.The amazon Rainforest has been around for about 55 million years old. The countries that have this forest within their borders are:

  1. Brazil
  2. Peru
  3. Columbia
  4. Venezuelia
  5. Ecuador
  6. Bolivia
  7. Guyana
  8. Suriname
  9. and French Guiana.

The Amazon covers almost all of the Amazon Basin: that is 2.7 million miles in size!

If the Amazon Rainforest was a country, it would be the 9th largest in the world!
